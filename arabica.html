    ABOUT ARABICA COFFEE

    Arabica coffee, also known as tea coffee, has the scientific name Coffea Arabica and is primarily grown in mountainous regions at altitudes between 1,000 – 1,500 meters. This coffee variety offers an incredibly diverse flavor profile, ranging from sweet and mild acidity to refreshing notes, with a characteristic rich aftertaste. Arabica beans contain a low caffeine content, only about 1 – 2%, but still provide enough energy to wake you up in the morning.

    ARABICA COFFEE TASTE

    According to experts, Arabica coffee is characterized by a mild acidity, subtle bitterness, and a uniquely balanced sweetness. This coffee variety boasts a diverse flavor profile, including floral notes, nutty undertones, chocolate, fruity hints, and citrus flavors, offering a truly distinctive taste experience. Additionally, the flavor of Arabica beans is influenced by the climate and soil conditions of different regions. For example, Central and South American Arabica is known for its rich chocolatey notes, while African Arabica is famous for its floral and fruity brightness.
