    Alongside Arabica, Robusta is also a well-known coffee variety that is widely consumed in Vietnam and many other countries around the world. With its distinct characteristics, it is not too difficult to recognize the unique flavor of this renowned coffee.

    ABOUT ROBUSTA COFFEE

    Robusta coffee belongs to the Coffea canephora species. Along with Coffea Arabica, it is one of the two most commercially cultivated coffee varieties worldwide. Robusta coffee has several outstanding advantages over Arabica, such as: High resistance to diseases like Coffee Leaf Rust, stem borers, and nematodes; Greater productivity compared to Arabica, making it a more efficient crop for farmers. These qualities contribute to Robusta’s widespread cultivation, especially in regions with lower altitudes and warmer climates.

    ROBUSTA COFFEE TASTE

    Robusta coffee has traditionally been considered less refined than Arabica in terms of flavor. It is generally stronger, more bitter, and more astringent compared to the smoother, more acidic, and complex taste of Arabica. Since Arabica is known for its higher acidity and richer flavor profile, it is often regarded as the premium coffee variety. However, Robusta’s boldness and high caffeine content make it a preferred choice for espresso blends and strong coffee lovers.
