    • GREEN COFFEE BEANS

    Green coffee beans, also known as raw coffee beans, are unroasted coffee beans that have not undergone heat processing such as roasting or grinding. These beans are obtained after harvesting and processing ripe, red coffee cherries. They naturally have a light green color and a moisture content of 12% to 13% (low moisture), allowing for long-term storage.

    PROCESSING OF GREEN COFFEE BEANS

    The processing of green coffee beans involves multiple steps, requiring extensive knowledge and high precision, making their market price relatively high. Arabica green coffee beans are considered premium and are priced higher than Robusta green coffee beans. Since they have not undergone roasting, their antioxidant properties remain intact, making them highly beneficial for weight loss and excess fat reduction.

    PROCESSING METHODS

    The processing of green coffee beans also depends on the coffee bean processing method. Currently, in Vietnam, there are three main processing methods: dry processing, wet processing, and honey processing (semi-washed processing).

    ARABICA AND ROBUSTA

    Green coffee beans are mainly classified into two types: Arabica and Robusta. Arabica Coffee has a delicate, aromatic flavor with a lower caffeine content. It is typically grown at altitudes between 800 to 1,200 meters, requiring more specific climate and soil conditions than Robusta. Some famous Arabica varieties worldwide include Caturra, Catuai, Typica, and Bourbon. Robusta Coffee has a bolder, stronger flavor than Arabica, with a higher caffeine content and better resistance to pests and diseases. It is usually cultivated at lower altitudes, ranging from 0 to 900 meters. Indonesia, Vietnam, and Brazil are three major producers of high-quality Robusta coffee, which is widely popular worldwide.
